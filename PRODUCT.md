# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Primary: **industrial procurement managers** at manufacturers and processors who buy bulk
commodity chemicals, solvents, and polymers. They arrive comparing suppliers, often under
time and cost pressure. They are evaluating whether Aurelis, a relatively new name, can be
trusted with a large order: price, reliability of supply, minimum order quantities, lead
times, logistics, and documentation. They skim. They want proof, not adjectives.

Secondary: R&D formulators and specialty buyers who need specs and grades, and other
traders/distributors buying for resale. The site should not alienate them, but procurement
is who we design for first.

## Product Purpose

A marketing and catalog website for Aurelis Chemicals, a bulk chemical trading and
distribution business covering solvents, polymers, and specialty chemicals. It exists to:

1. Establish Aurelis as a credible, capable supplier despite being a newer entrant.
2. Differentiate from the sea of look-alike distributors and dense listing-site traders.
3. Make a ~287-product catalog genuinely browsable and trustworthy.
4. Convert serious buyers into quote requests and direct sales contact.

Success looks like a procurement manager landing on the site, quickly believing Aurelis is
legitimate and well-run, finding the product they need, and requesting a quote.

## Brand Personality

Bold and modern. Aurelis is the agile newcomer modernizing a traditional, slow-moving
industry, and the brand should feel that way: sharp, current, and confident, not stodgy or
corporate-by-numbers. Three words: **confident, modern, dependable.**

Voice: direct, plain-spoken, expert. It states capability plainly and backs it with
specifics. It never oversells or pads. Emotional goal: reassurance (you can trust us with
this order) paired with a sense that this is a fresh, well-run operation, not your father's
chemical trader.

## Anti-references

- **Cheap, cluttered trader sites** (IndiaMART / TradeIndia listing dumps): dense, low-trust,
  dated, everything-shouting. This is the primary thing to avoid. A large catalog must feel
  organized and powerful, never like a data dump.
- **The navy + metallic-gold "premium corporate" template** the current site leans on. It is
  the category reflex for chemical/finance/industrial firms and reads as generic luxury.
  Aurelis should not look like every other distributor's idea of "premium."
- Cold, sterile enterprise corporate: stock-photo handshakes, lifeless blue gradients, beaker
  clip-art, soulless about-us pages.
- Flashy startup / web3 surface gloss: neon gradients, decorative glassmorphism everywhere,
  hype-y animation for its own sake.

## Design Principles

1. **Substance signals trust.** For procurement buyers, concrete proof builds credibility far
   faster than adjectives: real product data, specs, packaging, logistics, certifications,
   numbers. Show capability; never just claim it.
2. **Confidently distinctive.** As a newcomer, lean into an ownable, un-corporate identity
   rather than mimicking incumbents. Distinctiveness is a feature, not a risk. No navy + gold.
3. **Volume without clutter.** A 287-SKU catalog is a strength when it is structured,
   filterable, and scannable, and a liability when it is a dump. Hierarchy and restraint turn
   scale into authority.
4. **Built to skim.** Procurement managers scan under time pressure. Clarity, fast scanning,
   and obvious next steps always win over decoration. Bold can never cost legibility.
5. **Every page earns the quote.** Remove friction between a serious buyer and the sales team;
   each surface should move them one concrete step closer to making contact.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Sufficient text and UI contrast (especially against any bold or saturated
brand color), full keyboard navigation, visible focus states, and honored `prefers-reduced-motion`
(the site uses substantial scroll and entrance animation). Type must stay legible for older
buyers reading on mid-range office monitors.
